Built against PowerScribe's oldest complaints

“I dictated the exact same sentence I say twenty times a day. Different text. Again.”

Say it the same, get the same

PowerScribe's recognizer leans on a per-user voice profile that drifts, degrades, and eventually needs retraining. Dictum uses a modern transformer speech model (Parakeet-TDT) with deterministic decoding: no profile to train, no profile to corrupt — the same utterance produces the same text, with punctuation and measurements formatted the way radiologists write them. It decodes a five-second utterance in about half a second on an ordinary CPU.

“AutoText was clearly designed by someone who never dictated a case.”

Templates that fill themselves out

Import your own PowerScribe AutoText in one click — or start from the built-in template library. Every field is tab-navigable, picklists pop up with the default preselected, and voice commands drive it all: say “next field”, speak an option, keep dictating. Finished reports paste back into PowerScribe with the fields still live.

“It still doesn't know the words I use every single day.”

Autocomplete that learns you

The app quietly learns your vocabulary and phrasing from the reports you sign — locally, on disk you control. Type three letters and your own sentence finishes itself. A curated radiology corpus covers the rest, ranked by the section you're writing in.

Live mirror

The open report mirrors into the plugin in real time — fields, lists, formatting — and stays read-only unless you say otherwise. Dictation in PowerScribe always wins.

Crash-proof drafts

Work is autosaved every few seconds with full field fidelity. Kill the app, lose power, reboot — your draft comes back with tab navigation intact.

Made for reading rooms

A warm, low-glare theme that sits politely next to your PACS monitors, resizable, with your font and size remembered.
