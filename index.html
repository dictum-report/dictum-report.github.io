Sound familiar?

Built against PowerScribe's oldest complaints

“I dictated the exact same sentence I say twenty times a day. Different text. Again.”

Say it the same, get the same

Press F9 — or the PowerMic button already in your hand — and the words land straight in the open report. The engine is a modern transformer (Parakeet-TDT) with no voice profile to train, drift, or corrupt: the same utterance produces the same text. And it decodes about 10× faster than you speak on an ordinary CPU — you'll never watch the recognizer catch up.

“It still doesn't know the words I use every single day.”

Autocomplete, right in the report

Start a word in PowerScribe and a suggestion popup follows your caret — Tab accepts, Esc dismisses. Dictum quietly learns your vocabulary and phrasing from the reports you write — locally, on disk you control — so after a few days your own sentences finish themselves. A curated radiology corpus covers the rest.

“I just spent five minutes describing it. Now I get to summarize it all over again.”

Impressions draft themselves

Press F10 and an on-device medical language model drafts the IMPRESSION from the findings you just dictated — a diagnostic synthesis of your own words, never a finding you didn't make and never an unsolicited recommendation. It lands in the report as a draft for your review; you edit and sign exactly as you do today.

Drop-in, not rip-out

PowerScribe and Dragon stay exactly as they are. Every Dictum feature is a checkbox — untick them all and you're back to stock in one second.

PowerMic ready

The record button on your existing PowerMic drives Dictum — press-and-hold or toggle, your choice. No new hardware on the desk.

Made for reading rooms

A compact, always-on-top panel in a warm, low-glare theme that sits politely next to your PACS monitors and stays out of the way.

📄 Peer-reviewed roots: Dictum builds on its author's commentary in Radiology: Artificial Intelligence — “No Knew Typos: Modernizing Radiology Dictation” (RSNA, 2022) — on using transformer language models to catch the errors radiology speech recognition makes.

  1. Open Dictum.zip and run Dictum.exe inside — it sets itself up in seconds and starts. It finds your open PowerScribe report by itself.
